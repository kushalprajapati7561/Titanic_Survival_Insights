# Titanic_Survival_Insights
**Titanic Survival Insights**

This project analyzes the Titanic passenger dataset to identify the factors that influenced survival during the disaster. The dataset contains information such as passenger age, gender, ticket class, fare, embarkation port, and survival status. Data cleaning was performed by handling missing values, removing duplicates, and transforming variables into a suitable format for analysis.

A dashboard was created using Power BI to visualize key performance indicators (KPIs), including Total Passengers, Total Survivors, Survival Rate, and Total Deaths. Various charts and graphs were used to explore survival patterns across different categories. The analysis revealed that female passengers had a significantly higher survival rate than male passengers, indicating the impact of the “women and children first” evacuation policy. Passengers traveling in First Class were more likely to survive compared to those in Second and Third Class, suggesting that socioeconomic status played a major role in access to lifeboats and safety measures.

Age-based analysis showed that children had better survival chances than adults, while older passengers faced lower survival rates. Fare analysis indicated that passengers who paid higher ticket fares generally experienced better survival outcomes. Additionally, embarkation port analysis highlighted differences in survival rates among passengers boarding from different locations.

Interactive slicers and filters were added to the dashboard, enabling users to examine survival trends by gender, class, age group, and embarkation port. The project demonstrates the practical application of data cleaning, exploratory data analysis, and business intelligence techniques. Overall, the insights gained from the Titanic dataset provide a deeper understanding of how demographic and socioeconomic factors influenced passenger survival during one of history’s most famous maritime disasters.
